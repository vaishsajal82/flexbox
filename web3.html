WEB 3

Web3 (also known as Web 3.0)is an idea for a new iteration of the World Wide Web which incorporates concepts such as decentralization, blockchain technologies, tokenomics, and privacy-enhancing technologies.[4] This is distinct from Tim Berners-Lee's concept of the Semantic Web. Some technologists and journalists have contrasted it with Web 2.0, in which they say user-generated content is controlled by a small group of companies referred to as Big Tech.[5] The term "web3" was coined in 2014 by Ethereum co-founder Gavin Wood, and the idea gained interest in 2021 from cryptocurrency enthusiasts, large technology companies, and venture capital firms.[5][6] The concepts of web3 were first represented in 2013.[7][8] Critics have expressed concerns over the centralization of wealth to a small group of investors and individuals,or a loss of privacy due to more expansive data collection.[10] Billionaires like Elon Musk and Jack Dorsey have argued that web3 only serves as a buzzword or marketing term.

Properties of Web3.0

  1. Decentralisation
    • No single company or authority controls the network.
    • Data is stored across distributed nodes.
  2. Truthless and Permissionless
    • Users interact without needing intermediaries (banks, companies).
    • Anyone can join the network without approval.
    User Ownership
    • Users own their data, digital identity, and digital assets (via blockchain wallets).
  3. Transparency
    • Transactions and records are visible on public blockchains.
    • Increases Accountability
  4. Security Immutation
    • Data on the blockchain cannot be easily changed or hacked.
    • Cryptography ensures strong protection.
  5. Tokenization
    • Assets (money, art, identity) can be represented as tokens (NFTs, cryptocurrencies).
  6. Interoperability
    • Different blockchains, apps, and services can interact with each other.
  7. Smart Contracts
    • Self-executing programs that automate agreements without middlemen.
